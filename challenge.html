Our Mission: Answering the Stellar Stories Challenge

The Prompt

The 2025 NASA Space Apps Challenge asked us to write and illustrate a digital children's story explaining space weather and its varying impacts on different communities. We chose the challenging and unique perspective of an astronaut to give readers a first-hand look at the Sun's power.

Our Character: Astronaut Astra's Profile

Character Name: Astronaut Astra
Role: Commander aboard the International Space Station (ISS)
Connection to Space Weather: Astra experiences the effects firsthandâ€”from the need to shelter from solar flare radiation to navigating potential communication blackouts caused by Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs). Her experience bridges the gap between space physics and human life.

Objectives Met

  • Definition of Space Weather: Explained in the story through Astra's simple analogy of "The Sun's moods" (Flares are sneezes, CMEs are clouds).
  • Varying Impacts: We contrasted the direct risk to Astra (radiation) with three Earth-based impacts: A pilot losing radio communications, a farmer's GPS signal being disrupted, and the general public viewing the stunning aurora.
  • Defined Character: Astra is a fully developed persona who guides the narrative, making complex topics relatable for the target audience (Elementary School Students, ages 7-10).
